DID YOU KNOW?

  1. While the FDA uses 2,000 as the average number of calories a person should consume in a day, the specific number actually varies for each individual. Factors such as activity level, weight, age, gender, and other variables all need to be taken into consideration.

  2. It is believed that one-third of Americans are getting nearly half of their daily calories from junk food.

  3. Calories in our diet come from fat, protein, carbohydrates. Carbohydrates and protein contain 4 calories per gram. Fat has a whopping 9 calories per gram.

  4. Ideally, we should consume foods with a relatively high number of nutrients per calorie, or nutrient-dense foods. Otherwise, we are eating “empty calories”. Soft drinks are a good example of empty-calorie foods. Nutrient-dense foods include unprocessed food items, fruits, vegetables, etc.
